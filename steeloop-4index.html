The Problem

India's scrap market
is broken.

Steel is one of the hardest industries to decarbonise — and scrap recycling is one of the few near-term levers that actually works. But India's scrap supply chain is chaotic, informal, and trust-poor. Thousands of small holders and middlemen. No standard grading. Opaque pricing. Mills can't get consistent supply; small sellers can't get fair prices. The result is inefficiency, fraud, and a massive underuse of scrap as a climate solution.

Steeloop is building the infrastructure that fixes this — starting with cities like Kanpur, where the problem is visible and the opportunity is real.

Fragmented & Informal
Hundreds of thousands of scrap holders, dealers, and middlemen — with no shared standard for grading, documentation, or pricing. Every deal is a negotiation from scratch.
Quality Disputes & Fraud
Without objective grading, weight cheating, grade mixing, and payment disputes are routine. Low trust drives up costs for everyone and keeps the market inefficient.
Climate Potential Wasted
Scrap-based steelmaking emits up to 74% less CO₂ than ore-based production. But poor-quality, inconsistent scrap limits what mills can use — locking in higher-carbon routes by default.

The AI Stack

Five modules.
One operating system.

Each AI module does a distinct job — and together they form a continuous intelligence layer covering every step from scrap intake to mill delivery.

👁️
Vision Grading

Cameras and machine learning identify metal type, alloy composition, rust level, coatings, contamination, and shape — plates, beams, rebar, turnings, shredded, or mixed. Outputs a standard scrap grade and quality score with minimal human input. Reduces disputes and fraud at source.

COMPUTER VISION CNN MODEL XRF / SENSORS
📈
Pricing Engine

Ingests global and local scrap price data, mill demand patterns, local supply volumes, and logistics costs. Produces dynamic, location-aware price recommendations per grade — updated continuously so sellers get fair indicative quotes and Steeloop maintains healthy spreads.

REAL-TIME FEEDS 200+ CITIES GRADE-LEVEL
⚗️
Blend Optimisation

Algorithms suggest the optimal mix of scrap grades to hit a furnace's chemistry specification at minimum cost and lowest carbon footprint. Electric-arc furnaces — the low-carbon way to make steel — are particularly sensitive to scrap quality; better blends mean better yields.

EAF OPTIMISATION CHEMISTRY SPECS CARBON MIN.
🚛
Logistics & Routing

Route planning and load optimisation reduce empty truck runs, fuel consumption, and delays. The system groups loads by destination and grade, tracks vehicles in real time, and predicts ETAs — making the physical side of scrap movement as efficient as the digital side.

Fraud & Risk Detection

Detects suspicious weight differences, abnormal quality patterns, and unusual transaction behaviours across the supply chain. Scrap has historically been a low-trust market — this module is what lets Steeloop guarantee quality to buyers and protect sellers from being underpaid.

Cleaner Steel Production

Electric-arc furnaces (EAF) — which run primarily on scrap — emit a fraction of the CO₂ produced by blast furnaces. Steeloop increases the quality and volume of scrap that can feed EAFs, unlocking a faster, near-term decarbonisation pathway for heavy industry.

🤝
Inclusion for Small Players

India's informal scrap chain employs hundreds of thousands of people — many with no price visibility, no documentation, and no access to larger buyers. Steeloop formalises parts of this chain, giving small aggregators fair prices, digital records, and access to markets previously shut out of.

🌏
A Model for Emerging Markets

India's scrap market is a microcosm of similar markets across South and Southeast Asia, Africa, and Latin America. What works here — an AI-native infrastructure layer on top of a fragmented, informal supply chain — is replicable. Steeloop is designed from day one to scale across borders.
